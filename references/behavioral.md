# Behavioral / Leadership Interview — Grading Criteria

## What a Great Behavioral Interview Looks Like

The candidate should tell clear, specific stories that demonstrate the competency being tested. Answers should follow a strong narrative arc, show the candidate's individual contribution (not the team's), and end with a meaningful reflection on what they learned.

---

## Grading Dimensions (each scored 0–10)

### 1. STAR Structure (Weight: High)
**What to look for:**
- Did the candidate use a clear Situation → Task → Action → Result structure?
- Was the story easy to follow?
- Was appropriate time spent on each part (most time on Action, not setup)?

**Strong**: Clear STAR arc. Situation is brief (20%), Task is clear, Action is detailed (50%), Result is quantified.
**Weak**: Rambling setup, vague action, no result. Or skips directly to action without context.

---

### 2. Specificity (Weight: High)
**What to look for:**
- Did the candidate give a specific example from their actual experience?
- Were there concrete details (team size, timeline, company context, constraints)?
- Did they avoid speaking in generalities ("I always try to...")?

**Strong**: "At Company X in Q3 2023, I was leading a 4-person team and we had 6 weeks to ship Y."
**Weak**: Generic answer without a specific situation, or overly hypothetical.

---

### 3. Individual Contribution (Weight: High)
**What to look for:**
- Was it clear what *the candidate* did vs. what the team did?
- Did they use "I" for their actions and "we" appropriately for team context?
- Did they claim appropriate credit without overstating?

**Strong**: Clear distinction between candidate's actions and the team's. Ownership is evident.
**Weak**: Only says "we did X" — impossible to tell what the candidate personally did.

---

### 4. Impact & Results (Weight: High)
**What to look for:**
- Did the candidate quantify the outcome where possible?
- Did the result connect back to the business or team goal?
- Was the magnitude of impact clear?

**Strong**: "As a result, we reduced churn by 12%, which contributed to $2M in retained ARR."
**Weak**: "It went well" or "the team was happy." No numbers, no business connection.

---

### 5. Competency Fit (Weight: High)
**What to look for:**
- Did the story actually demonstrate the competency the question was asking about?
- Was the answer relevant to the question, or did it drift?

**Strong**: Story clearly demonstrates the asked-for competency (e.g., if asked about conflict, the story involves resolving a genuine conflict).
**Weak**: Story is strong but doesn't answer the actual question asked.

---

### 6. Reflection & Learning (Weight: Medium)
**What to look for:**
- Did the candidate reflect on what they learned or what they'd do differently?
- Did the reflection show genuine self-awareness?
- Did they avoid being defensive about mistakes?

**Strong**: "Looking back, I'd have involved the stakeholder earlier. That would have saved 2 weeks of rework."
**Weak**: "I wouldn't change anything" or no reflection offered.

---

### 7. Complexity & Stakes (Weight: Medium)
**What to look for:**
- Was the example appropriately challenging? (Not trivially easy)
- Were there real tradeoffs, constraints, or ambiguity involved?
- Did the story have stakes?

**Strong**: Example involves genuine difficulty — conflict, ambiguity, limited resources, or failure.
**Weak**: Example is too minor to be impressive (e.g., "I organized a lunch" for a leadership question).

---

### 8. Conciseness (Weight: Medium)
**What to look for:**
- Did the candidate stay focused and avoid going over ~3 minutes per answer?
- Did they cut unnecessary tangents?
- Did they answer the question directly?

**Strong**: Answer is crisp and complete. No rambling.
**Weak**: Answer takes too long, includes irrelevant details, or loses the interviewer.

---

## Common Failure Modes

- **Team answers**: Only saying "we" — interviewer can't tell what the candidate did
- **No result**: Strong setup and action but no outcome stated
- **Too generic**: "I'm a strong communicator" with no supporting story
- **Wrong story**: Story doesn't illustrate the competency that was asked about
- **No reflection**: Treats every outcome as a success with nothing learned
- **Too long**: Over-explaining the situation and running out of time for the action/result

---

## Score Benchmarks

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | Excellent: specific, compelling story, clear individual contribution, quantified impact, genuine reflection |
| 7–8 | Good: clear story, mostly specific, some quantification, minor gaps in structure or reflection |
| 5–6 | Borderline: story is present but too vague, missing result, or doesn't match the question |
| 3–4 | Weak: generic or hypothetical, no result, or poor STAR structure |
| 0–2 | Not ready: no story, answered with opinions instead of experience |
